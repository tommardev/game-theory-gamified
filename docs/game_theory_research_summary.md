# Game Theory Research: Summary

**Game theory** is the study of mathematical models of strategic interactions among rational decision-makers. It applies when the outcome of your decision depends on the decisions made by others.

### Key Takeaways
- **Players, Strategies, and Payoffs:** The fundamental elements of any game.
- **Nash Equilibrium:** A state where no player has an incentive to deviate from their chosen strategy after considering an opponent's choice.
- **Game Types:** Can be cooperative (agreements allowed) or non-cooperative, zero-sum (one wins, one loses) or non-zero-sum, and sequential or simultaneous.
- **Broad Applications:** Used extensively in economics, political science, psychology, biology, and computer science to model complex behaviors and predict outcomes.
